Experience

March 2016 - Present

Librarian, Southern Medical Program, UBC Okanagan

This position has grown and evolved to include a number of responsibilities from reference to undergraduate medicine Case Based Learning tutoring, supervising student research projects, managing the UBCO makerspace, supporting faculty research, and coordinating the Fostering Open Science Initiative at UBCO.

Data & Research Data Management

  • Member of the Library’s Research Data Management team supporting the Library’s efforts to help researchers in the context of RDM.
  • Collaborate with colleagues across the institution and the country in environmental scans of researchers’ practices and needs and solutions to data deposit and discovery.
  • Develop and deliver class instruction and workshops on principles of data management, discovery, and deposit.
  • Work with partners in Advanced Research Computing (ARC) and Office of Research Services to align the processes of Data Management Plans and research ethics requirements.

Open Science and Digital Scholarship

  • Member of the Fostering Open Science Initiative, a two year $100 000 strategically funded initiative to promote and enable best practices and principles in Open Science.
  • Collaborate with faculty across disciplines, ARC, IT and VP Research to help understand and implement strategies to enable researcher best practices in the context of Open from study design to dissemination of results and management of research inputs and outputs.
  • Collaborate with faculty, lab managers, library colleagues, and VP Academic to integrate Open Science principles and practices into the undergraduate classroom.
  • Maintain abreast of and consult on trends in data and digital scholarship including software and hardware needs and availability to support data analysis and visualisation and standards and storage options to ensure data accessibility.
  • Member of the Library’s Centre for Scholarly Communication (CSC) working group, participating in initial environmental scans for the development of the Library’s delivery of digital scholarship services and currently working to build out these services.
  • Led UBC’s partnering with OSF to become Canada’s first post secondary institution to offer researchers a UBC OSF Institutional platform on which to collaborate and share their research.

Reference, Liaison, and Teaching and Learning

  • Support teaching, learning and research across UBC’s distributed Undergraduate Medical Education Program (UGME) as well as in Human Kinetics and Biology at the Okanagan campus.

  • W1, 2018, W2 2020, listed W2 2021 - Co-instruct HMKN 545C Introduction to Narrative, Scoping, and Systematic Reviews in Health and Exercise Sciences. Graduate seminar covering all aspects of the review process, including review types, protocol development and registration, risk of bias and quality assessment of primary and secondary research, statistical analyses used in systematic reviews and meta-analyses, advanced searching, and coding methods.

  • Integrated across the HMKN undergraduate curriculum, working with instructors to scaffold library skills and information literacy across the four year program.

  • Work with colleagues on the introduction of Open Science principles and practices complimenting existing class syllabi and assignments in undergraduate Biology.

  • Support the UGME’s Case Based Learning (CBL) as a tutor for the first two years of the program.

  • Annually participate as a facilitator in UBC Health’s Integrated Curriculum delivery of their medical ethics modules.

  • Provide workshops and class instruction on the many aspects of scholarly communication including the publishing landscape, RDM, discoverability and retrievability of outputs including DOIs and ORCIDs, and platforms to support the research life cycle including OSF and Dataverse.

  • Participated in the development, coordination, and delivery of the RDM Fall Series 2020, a 3 week RDM series covering all aspects of data in the research life cycle.

  • Currently co-developing an introductory series on statistical software (R and SPSS) for the CSC.

Outreach and Communication

  • Co-led the production and launch of a podcast series featuring UBC Okanagan faculty and researchers critically discussing their work and practices within the academy, promoting open dialogue around the research paradigm.
  • Conducted environmental scans and interviews to inform the offering of public library lending at UBCO through Okanagan Regional Library.
  • Annually co-chair Science Literacy Week, exploring novel ways of connecting with library users and campus partners in the pursuit of enhanced science literacy.

Supervisory & Coordination

  • Supervise student research projects through the UGME. Currently supervising one student examining facilitators and barriers to supports for survivors of sexual violence in Kelowna.
  • Co-supervise one graduate student to support the Fostering Open Science at UBC Initiative.
  • 2017-2019 managed and coordinated makerspace UBCO working with a broad range of faculties and upwards of nine student reports to promote creative engagement and prototyping across disciplines.
  • 2016-2017 co-supervised one library staff position in relation to their duties to the Southern Medical Program.

Current Research

  • Participate in the development, implementation and authoring of several reviews in the health sciences in the areas of behaviour change, physical activity, and partnership research. A list of relevant publications, protocols, and conference presentations emanating from this research can be found below.
  • With colleagues at ECRI and Cochrane, currently developing validated search strategies leveraging the National Library of Medicine’s Edirect Utilities and R for natural language processing to limit bias and improve transparency and computational reproducibility of our outputs.
